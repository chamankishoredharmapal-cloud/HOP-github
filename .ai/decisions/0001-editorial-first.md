# ADR-0001: Editorial-First Design

**Status**: Accepted  
**Date**: 2026-07-19  
**Author**: AI Architecture Team  

## Context
The House of Padmavati brand demands a luxury experience. Traditional e-commerce layouts (dense grids, heavy CTAs, aggressive promotions) conflict with the editorial, magazine-like aesthetic required for a heritage luxury saree brand.

## Decision
Adopt an editorial-first design approach:
- Layouts mimic luxury editorial magazines (spreads, pull quotes, full-bleed imagery)
- Content hierarchy: story > product. Products are contextualized within narratives (collections, craftsmanship stories, style guides)
- Whitespace is generous; nothing feels cramped
- CTAs are understated, not aggressive ("Add to your collection" not "BUY NOW")
- Product pages prioritize visual storytelling over data density

## Consequences
### Positive
- Stronger brand alignment with luxury positioning
- Higher engagement and time-on-site
- Editorial content drives organic SEO
- Differentiates from mass-market e-commerce

### Negative
- Lower product density per page (fewer products visible above fold)
- Requires high-quality editorial photography and copy
- May reduce conversion rate for price-sensitive shoppers (acceptable — luxury segment)
- More content creation overhead

## Compliance
- All pages follow editorial layout patterns
- Product pages include narrative sections (craftsmanship, styling, heritage)
- Hero sections use full-bleed editorial imagery
- Collections have curator's notes and thematic descriptions
