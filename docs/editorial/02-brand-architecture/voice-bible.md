# Voice Bible

## Voice Definition

HOP's voice is warm, reserved, and precise. It speaks the way a trusted friend who knows cloth deeply would speak — with confidence that never becomes arrogance, with warmth that never becomes familiarity, with knowledge that never becomes pedantry.

## Voice Dimensions

### Primary Dimensions

| Dimension | Scale | Position | Description |
|-----------|-------|----------|-------------|
| **Formality** | 1 (Casual) – 5 (Formal) | 3 | Warm but not casual. Refined but not stiff. Approachable but never slangy. |
| **Warmth** | 1 (Cold) – 5 (Warm) | 4 | Intimate, personal, never corporate. Speaks to one woman, not a demographic. |
| **Sensory Density** | 1 (Sparse) – 5 (Dense) | 4–5 | Rich with texture, light, weight, colour, sound. The reader should feel the cloth. |
| **Technical Depth** | 1 (None) – 5 (Deep) | 2 | Craft details are present but never dominate. Available on demand via accordion, never forced into narrative flow. |
| **Urgency** | 1 (None) – 5 (High) | 0 | No scarcity, no countdowns, no pressure. The saree will wait. |
| **Imperative Force** | 1 (Gentle) – 5 (Commanding) | 1 | Suggest, invite, welcome — never command. "You might begin with..." not "Start here." |

### Secondary Dimensions

| Dimension | Scale | Position | Description |
|-----------|-------|----------|-------------|
| **Abstract vs Concrete** | 1 (Abstract) – 5 (Concrete) | 4 | Prefer specific things over general qualities. "The light in Pondicherry at 5:47 AM" not "a beautiful setting." |
| **Emotional vs Rational** | 1 (Emotional) – 5 (Rational) | 2 | Emotion leads. Reason supports. Feel first, justify second. |
| **Dense vs Sparse** | 1 (Sparse) – 5 (Dense) | 3 | Every word earns its place. No padding. No word count targets. |
| **Short vs Long Sentences** | 1 (Short) – 5 (Long) | 3 | Vary. Three short, one long, repeat. Rhythm over consistency. |

## Sentence Architecture

### Length Rules

- Average sentence length: 12–18 words
- Maximum sentence length: 25 words (exceptions only in policy pages)
- Vary length deliberately: three short sentences followed by one longer sentence creates rhythm
- No two consecutive sentences should have the same structure

### Paragraph Rules

- 2–4 sentences per paragraph
- One idea per paragraph
- No transition paragraphs — if the idea changes, start a new paragraph
- First line of a paragraph should be short and declarative

### Opening and Closing

- Start sections with a short, declarative sentence that lands
- End sections with a sentence that resolves — a conclusion, not a fade
- The last sentence of a piece should leave the reader in a quiet place
- Never open with a question. Never close with a question.

### Rhythm

```
Short sentence.
Medium sentence that extends the thought.
Longer sentence that adds a detail or sensory observation.
Short sentence that lands.
```

Example:

> The zari catches the morning light. Not the harsh noon light, but the soft gold of early day, when everything is still possible. This is when a weave reveals itself — not under a studio lamp, but in the quiet hour between five and seven, when the house has not yet woken. This is when the cloth speaks.

### Prohibition

| Pattern | Rule | Example ✗ | Example ✓ |
|---------|------|-----------|-----------|
| Questions | Do not address the reader directly with questions | "Have you ever wondered what makes a saree special?" | "The finest sarees reveal themselves slowly." |
| Exclamations | Never use exclamation marks | "Shop now!" | "Browse the collection." |
| Imperatives | Avoid commanding the reader | "Buy this saree." | "This saree belongs to quiet weddings." |
| Clichés | No fashion or luxury clichés | "Elevate your wardrobe." | "This drape settles at the hip like a held breath." |
| Self-praise | Do not describe HOP as "luxury" or "premium" | "Experience luxury." | "Handwoven on a pit loom in Molakalmuru." |

## Pronouns

| Pronoun | Usage | Example |
|---------|-------|---------|
| **We / Our / Us** | The house speaking as one voice. Always collective, never singular. | "We make sarees the way they were once made for our own homes." |
| **You / Your** | The woman we are speaking to. Direct but gentle. | "Your saree will be wrapped in jasmine paper." |
| **She / Her** | The wearer (when not addressing the reader directly). | "She wears it like she was born in it." |
| **The House** | Third-person reference to HOP. | "A note from the house." |

**Never use:** "I" (singular founder voice), "one" (too formal), "they" (plural, generic — use "you" or "she").
