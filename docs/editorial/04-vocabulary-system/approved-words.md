# Approved Words

Words organized by category. These are the building blocks of all HOP writing. Use them freely. If you need a word not on this list, consult the Forbidden Words list first. If it is not forbidden, it may still need approval from the Editorial Lead.

## Luxury (Implied, Not Stated)

Words that signal luxury through specificity and restraint — not through claiming luxury directly.

| Word | Why It Works | Usage Example |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| Quiet | Signals exclusivity through absence of noise | "A quiet weave for a quiet ceremony." |
| Considered | Implies intention and care | "Every thread is considered." |
| Refined | Suggests discernment without arrogance | "A refined hand, passed through generations." |
| Restrained | Confidence expressed through limitation | "The border is restrained — a single line of zari." |
| Intentional | Opposite of accidental | "The colour is intentional. It mirrors the monsoon coast." |
| Discerning | For the woman who chooses carefully | "A saree for the discerning eye." |
| Gentle | Soft power, not aggression | "The drape is gentle. It follows the body, never fights it." |
| Deliberate | Every detail is a choice | "The placement of the zari is deliberate — not one thread wasted." |

## Craft

Precise, specific, honest. These words describe the reality of handwoven cloth without marketing inflation.

| Word | Why It Works | Usage Example |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| Loom | The object. Specific and honest. | "Twenty-one days on the loom." |
| Hand | The human element. | "A single hand guides each thread." |
| Weave | Verb and noun. Active and specific. | "The weave opens and closes like a breath." |
| Thread | The basic unit of cloth. | "The thread carries the dye differently in winter." |
| Zari | Specific. Precious. Real. | "Pure zari, hand-spun, genuine." |
| Pallu | The part of the saree that signals identity. | "The pallu falls over the shoulder like water." |
| Drape | How the cloth behaves on the body. | "The drape settles at the hip." |
| Tension | The weaver's skill. | "The tension is adjusted twice an hour." |
| Rhythm | The pattern of making. | "The rhythm of the shuttle — back and forth, back and forth." |
| Weight | A sensory quality and a value signal. | "The weight of pure mulberry silk." |
| Fold | The action that preserves. | "Fold it in muslin. Refold each season." |
| Border | A defining structural element. | "The temple border rises from the weave." |
| Body | The main field of the saree. | "The body is a single unbroken weave." |

## Emotion (Understated)

Restrained emotional vocabulary. Avoid intensity. Prefer quiet depth.

| Word | Why It Works | Usage Example |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| Quiet | Versatile. Means what it says. | "A quiet sort of beauty." |
| Still | A moment held. | "Stillness is part of the weave." |
| Soft | Sensory and emotional. | "Soft as morning light through linen." |
| Warm | Temperature and feeling. | "Warm like the afternoon sun on a cotton saree." |
| Deep | Colour and meaning. | "A deep teal that reads almost black in low light." |
| Gentle | Kind without being saccharine. | "A gentle ceremony for a quiet wedding." |
| Held | Carried, supported, remembered. | "The cloth is held by hands that have woven for forty years." |
| Carried | Both literal and emotional. | "She carries the weave like a second skin." |
| Belongs | Ownership without possessiveness. | "This drape belongs to winter evenings." |
| Remembers | Cloth as keeper of memory. | "The fold remembers the hand that made it." |

## Time (Slow, Generational)

Words that establish HOP's relationship with time — slow, patient, generational.

| Word | Why It Works | Usage Example |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| Season | Natural time, not commercial. | "The dye takes on a different character each season." |
| Generation | Human time. | "A technique passed through four generations." |
| Morning | Specific and sensory. | "The morning light reads the weave differently." |
| Evening | Closure, rest, ceremony. | "An evening saree — deep, still, intentional." |
| Hour | Measured, human-scale time. | "Sixty hours per drape." |
| Day | The unit of weaving. | "Twenty-one days on the loom." |
| Year | Long enough to matter. | "A saree that outlives its first year, and its first wearer." |
| Slowly | The adverb of the house. | "Slowly, without noise." |
| Continues | Not starts or finishes — continues. | "The tradition continues in a small village in Karnataka." |
| Passed | Generational handover. | "Passed from mother to daughter, fold by fold." |

## Color

Brand-specific color vocabulary. Never use generic color names.

| Brand Name | Visual Description | When to Use |
|------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Jasmine | Warm off-white, the colour of the jasmine flower at dawn | Page backgrounds, space, breath |
| Coastal Teal | Deep blue-green, the colour of the Bay of Bengal at certain hour | Primary accent, depth, intention |
| Golden Sand | Warm beige-gold, the colour of Tamil Nadu river sand | Secondary accent, warmth, tradition |
| Sakura Blush | Soft pink-brown, the colour of the cherry blossom that appears for a week | Tertiary accent, softness, imperfection |
| Ink Charcoal | Almost-black with warmth | Body text, serious elements |
| Jasmine Mist | The lightest jasmine, almost white | Background variation |
| Ink | The brand's neutral black | Primary text |
| Teal (Deep) | Darker coastal teal | Primary buttons, links |

**Never use:** Generic colour names like "beige," "cream," "blue-green," "pink," "grey," "off-white." Always map to the brand palette.

## Human

Words that keep the brand human-scale.

| Word | Why It Works | Usage Example |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| Woman | The wearer, the weaver, the reader. | "She wears it like she was born in it." |
| Hand | The human element in making. | "The hand that wove this has been weaving for thirty years." |
| Mother | The first teacher, the first giver. | "Her mother wore one like this." |
| Daughter | Continuance. | "To be passed to a daughter, and hers after." |
| Grandmother | Depth of generational memory. | "Her grandmother taught her the temple border." |
| Weaver | A specific person with a specific skill. | "Gangamma, a weaver in Molakalmuru." |
| Sister | Kinship without being literal. | "Sister weaves beside sister." |
| House | The HOP identity. | "A note from the house." |

## Place

Specific geography that roots the brand in real locations.

| Word | Region | Usage |
|------|--------|-------|
| Molakalmuru | Karnataka | Temple border silk weaving cluster |
| Ilkal | Karnataka | Distinctive cotton-silk weave with tope-teni pallu |

| Karnataka | State | Primary weaving region |
| Tamil Nadu | State | Secondary weaving region, loom infrastructure |
| Kanchipuram | Tamil Nadu | Reference for silk weaving tradition (context, not product) |
| Coastal | Geography | The brand's atmospheric identity |
