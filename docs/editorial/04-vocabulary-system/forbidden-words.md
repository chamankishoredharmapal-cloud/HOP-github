# Forbidden Words

These words must never appear in HOP content. They damage the brand by signalling mass-market ecommerce, generic luxury, urgency, or inauthenticity.

## Luxury Clichés

Words that claim luxury without earning it. These are the most damaging because they signal the opposite of what HOP stands for — they sound like every other brand trying to sound premium.

| Word | Replace With | Why |
|------|-------------|-----|
| Luxury | (describe what makes it exceptional instead) | Overused to the point of meaninglessness. Claims what must be earned. |
| Premium | (be specific about material or process) | Same as above. A $20 product can be called "premium." |
| Exquisite | (avoid entirely) | Overused in fashion marketing. Sounds insincere. |
| Opulent | (avoid entirely) | Too heavy, too Mughal-miniature. Not HOP's quiet aesthetic. |
| Lavish | (avoid entirely) | Opposite of HOP's restraint. |
| Glamorous | (avoid entirely) | Hollywood, not handloom. |
| Extravagant | (avoid entirely) | HOP is considered, not extravagant. |
| Plush | (avoid entirely) | Wrong texture association for sarees. |
| Sumptuous | (avoid entirely) | Food word applied to cloth. Overused. |

## Generic Praise

Words that say nothing. The reader's brain skips over them.

| Word | Replace With | Why |
|------|-------------|-----|
| Beautiful | (describe what is beautiful about it) | The most meaningless word in fashion. Tells nothing. |
| Stunning | (describe what is striking) | Implies surprise, not appreciation. |
| Gorgeous | (avoid entirely) | Overused, empty. |
| Lovely | (avoid entirely) | Too mild for a premium brand. |
| Amazing | (avoid entirely) | Internet hyperbole. |
| Incredible | (avoid entirely) | Same as above. |
| Wonderful | (avoid entirely) | Generic positivity. |
| Fabulous | (avoid entirely) | Drag culture, not saree culture. |

## Urgency & Scarcity

Words that create pressure. HOP never pressures.

| Word | Replace With | Why |
|------|-------------|-----|
| Hurry | (never use) | Undermines quiet luxury. |
| Limited edition | This season's weave, a small batch | Artificial scarcity. |
| Only X left | (never show inventory pressure) | Ecommerce manipulation. |
| Last chance | (never use) | Opposite of generational thinking. |
| While stocks last | (never use) | Same as above. |
| Don't miss out | (never use) | FOMO-driven. HOP does not use FOMO. |
| Exclusive | (avoid) | Overused. If something is exclusive, the reader should infer it. |
| One-time | (never use) | Fake urgency. |
| Limited time | (never use) | HOP never runs timed offers. |

## Marketing & Ecommerce Language

Words that sound like a sales script.

| Word | Replace With | Why |
|------|-------------|-----|
| Shop now | Browse the collection | Too transactional. |
| Buy now | Add to your collection | Same. |
| Sale | (never use) | HOP never runs sales. |
| Discount | (never use) | HOP never discounts. |
| Offer | (avoid) | Promotional tone. |
| Deal | (never use) | Mass-market. |
| Free shipping | Included shipping | Makes the shipping sound like a favour. |
| Checkout | Complete your order | Transactional. |
| Add to cart | Add to bag | "Cart" sounds like a supermarket. "Bag" sounds like fashion. |
| Subscribe | (avoid) | Too newsletter-industry. Use "follow the journal." |
| Sign up | (avoid) | Same. |

## Fashion Industry Clichés

Words that sound like every other fashion brand.

| Word | Replace With | Why |
|------|-------------|-----|
| Wardrobe essential | (avoid entirely) | Cliché. |
| Statement piece | (avoid entirely) | Cliché. |
| Must-have | (avoid entirely) | Aggressive. |
| Elevate your style | (avoid entirely) | The most fashion-cliché sentence possible. |
| Investment piece | (avoid, use "heirloom" instead) | Financial language applied to clothing feels cold. |
| Transitional piece | (avoid) | Stylist jargon. |
| Capsule wardrobe | (avoid) | Trend language. |
| Season's must-have | (avoid entirely) | Aggressive + cliché. |
| Fashion-forward | (avoid entirely) | HOP is not fashion-forward. It is outside fashion. |
| Trend | (never use) | HOP does not follow trends. |
| Trending | (never use) | Same. |
| It-saree | (avoid entirely) | Internet language. |

## Empty Intensifiers

Words that add emotion without substance.

| Word | Replace With | Why |
|------|-------------|-----|
| Absolutely | (remove) | Weakens the word it modifies. |
| Truly | (remove) | Implies the brand sometimes lies. |
| Really | (remove) | Same. |
| Very | (remove) | Lazy intensifier. |
| Extremely | (remove) | Same. |
| Incredibly | (remove) | Same. |
| Perfect | (avoid) | Nothing is perfect. "This belongs to..." is more honest. |
| Ultimate | (avoid) | Claims too much. |
| Best | (avoid unless comparative with specific referent) | "Best saree" is meaningless. "Best for winter weddings" is specific. |
| Pure | (use only for certified materials like "pure mulberry silk") | Overused as a general quality claim. |

## Abstract Branding

Words that try to create meaning through abstraction rather than specificity.

| Word | Replace With | Why |
|------|-------------|-----|
| Lifestyle | (avoid entirely) | Silicon Valley branding language. |
| Experience | (avoid for marketing use) | "The HOP experience" means nothing. |
| Journey | (avoid for marketing use) | Same. Overused in brand strategy. |
| Community | (avoid pushing) | Let it emerge. Do not claim it. |
| Ecosystem | (avoid entirely) | Tech word. |
| Solution | (avoid entirely) | A saree is not a solution to a problem. |
| Empower | (avoid entirely) | Overused in women's marketing. A saree does not empower a woman. She empowers herself. |
| Celebrate | (avoid for commercial use) | "Celebrate womanhood" is every brand's language. |

## Technical Overprecision in Narrative

These are fine in System 2 accordion sections. Forbidden in narrative (System 1) sections.

| Word | Allowed In | Forbidden In |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Momme | Accordion only | Sensory story |
| GSM | Accordion only | Sensory story |
| SKU | Studio only | Storefront |
| SKU number | Studio only | Storefront |
| SKU code | Studio only | Storefront |
| Unit | Studio only | Storefront (use "saree" or "drape") |
| Inventory | Studio only | Storefront |

## Visual & Structural Forbidden

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| Exclamation marks | Never. Not in copy, not in CTAs, not in emails. |
| All-caps sentences | Never. Not for emphasis. |
| Ampersands in body copy | Allowed only in navigation labels and taglines ("Sarees · Stories · You") |
| Double spaces after periods | Never. |
| Straight quotes | Use curly quotes. |
| Emoji | Never in body copy. |
