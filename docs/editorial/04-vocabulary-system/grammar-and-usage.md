# Grammar and Usage

These rules govern the mechanics of every sentence written for House of Padmavati. Consistency in grammar is not pedantry — it is part of the brand's precision and care.

## Punctuation

### Periods

- Use in all complete sentences. HOP does not use headline-style capitalization without punctuation.
- No double spaces after periods. Single space only.

### Exclamation Marks

- **Never use.** Not in body copy, not in CTAs, not in emails, not in social captions.
- If you feel an exclamation mark is necessary, rewrite the sentence.
- The only exception is customer service correspondence where the customer has used an exclamation mark.

### Commas

- **Oxford comma required.** In a list of three or more items, include the comma before "and" or "or."
  - ✓ "The saree carries the morning light, the afternoon heat, and the evening cool."
  - ✗ "The saree carries the morning light, the afternoon heat and the evening cool."

### Em Dashes

- Use em dashes (—) for interruptions, parenthetical thoughts, or emphasis.
- Use with a space on both sides.
  - ✓ "The cloth — this one — has been folded for thirty years."
  - ✗ "The cloth - this one - has been folded."
  - ✗ "The cloth--this one--has been folded."
- Keyboard shortcut: Alt+0151 (Windows), Option+Shift+Minus (Mac).

### En Dashes

- Use en dashes (–) for ranges and connections between two things.
  - ✓ "5–7 business days"
  - ✓ "The Pondicherry–Molakalmuru route"
- Keyboard shortcut: Alt+0150 (Windows), Option+Minus (Mac).

### Hyphens

- Use for compound modifiers before nouns.
  - ✓ "A hand-woven saree"
  - ✓ "A well-considered drape"
- Do not use hyphen after adverbs ending in -ly.
  - ✓ "A carefully woven border"
  - ✗ "A carefully-woven border"

### Quotation Marks

- Use curly (smart) quotes, not straight quotes.
  - ✓ "A whisper to go with the weave."
  - ✗ "A whisper to go with the weave."
- Single quotes for quotes within quotes:
  - ✓ "She said, 'This weave belongs to winter,' and folded it."

### Apostrophes

- Use curly apostrophe.
  - ✓ "The weaver's hands"
  - ✗ "The weaver's hands" (this is the straight apostrophe — incorrect)

### Colons

- Capitalize the word after a colon if it begins a complete sentence.
  - ✓ "The reason: Slow making."
  - ✓ "There is only one rule: the cloth comes first."
- Lowercase after colon if the colon introduces a list or fragment.
  - ✓ "Three things: thread, tension, and time."

### Semicolons

- Use sparingly. Prefer separate sentences or an em dash.

### Ellipses

- Use rarely. An ellipsis suggests hesitation or trailing off. HOP's voice is confident.
- When used, three dots only (not four). Space before and after.
  - ✓ "She unfolded it slowly ... and said nothing."

### Bullet Points

- Capitalize the first word of each bullet.
- End each bullet with a period if it is a complete sentence.
- End each bullet with no punctuation if fragments.
- Be consistent within each list.

## Capitalization

### Titles and Headlines

- Sentence case for headlines, not title case.
  - ✓ "How morning light reads a weave."
  - ✗ "How Morning Light Reads A Weave"
- Exception: Collection names, product names, and proper names use title case.
  - ✓ "The Kalyani Collection"
  - ✓ "House of Padmavati"

### Brand Name

- Always "House of Padmavati" (capital H, capital P).
- Abbreviation "HOP" is allowed in internal documentation and order numbers only. Never on the storefront.
  - ✓ "House of Padmavati" (storefront)
  - ✓ "Order HOP-20260724-XXXXXX" (order number)

### Titles (job, honorific)

- Lowercase: "the weaver," "the founder," "the editorial lead"
- Capitalize when part of a name: "Weaver Gangamma"

## Formatting

### Numbers

- Spell out one through nine. Use numerals for 10 and above.
  - ✓ "Sixty hours per drape. 12 momme silk."
  - ✓ "Five sarees, 21 days each."
- Use commas in numbers with four or more digits.
  - ✓ "1,200"
  - ✓ "24,000"
- Currency: "₹ 24,000" (space after symbol). Use Indian number formatting (lakhs, crores) only where appropriate.
  - ✓ "₹ 24,000" (not ₹24,000)
  - ✓ "The investment of a lifetime" (not the numerical framing in narrative)

### Dates

- Format: "12 July 2026" (no commas).
  - ✓ "Published 12 July 2026"
  - ✗ "July 12, 2026"
  - ✗ "12th July 2026"

### Time

- Use lowercase with periods: "a.m.," "p.m."
  - ✓ "5:47 a.m."
  - ✗ "5:47 AM"

### Measurements

- Use numerals: "5.5 metres," "12 momme"
- Use abbreviated units in functional contexts only: "5.5m" (technical), but "five and a half metres" (narrative)

## Usage Rules

### That vs. Which

- **That** introduces a restrictive clause (essential to the meaning).
  - ✓ "The saree that arrived yesterday is a Molakalmuru." (specific saree)
- **Which** introduces a non-restrictive clause (adds information, preceded by comma).
  - ✓ "The Molakalmuru, which is woven in Karnataka, carries a temple border."

### Who vs. That

- **Who** for people. **That** for objects.
  - ✓ "The woman who wove this."
  - ✓ "The thread that carries the dye."

### Less vs. Fewer

- **Less** for uncountable quantities.
  - ✓ "Less thread was used."
- **Fewer** for countable items.
  - ✓ "Fewer sarees were released this month."

### Among vs. Between

- **Among** for three or more.
  - ✓ "Among the five collections, Kalyani is the quietest."
- **Between** for two.
  - ✓ "Between the looms and the dye vats, the studio is a world of its own."

### Since vs. Because

- **Since** for time. **Because** for causality.
  - ✓ "Since 2024, HOP has released twelve collections."
  - ✓ "Because the loom was silent, the weaver rested."

### Into vs. In To

- **Into** indicates movement toward the inside.
  - ✓ "Fold the saree into muslin."
- **In to** is "in" + "to" (separate words).
  - ✓ "She came in to see the weave."

## Sentence Construction

### Starting a Sentence

- Avoid starting with "There is" or "There are."
  - ✗ "There is a saree that carries..."
  - ✓ "A saree that carries..."
- Avoid starting with "It is" when "it" has no referent.
  - ✗ "It is important to note that..."
  - ✓ (Remove the construction entirely.)

### Ending a Sentence

- End with a content word, not a preposition.
  - ✗ "This is the weave she belongs to."
  - ✓ "This weave belongs to her."
- Do not end with a trailing "..." unless indicating a deliberate pause (rare).

### Passive Voice

- Prefer active voice. Passive voice is acceptable only when the actor is unknown or irrelevant.
  - ✓ Active: "The weaver adjusts the tension twice an hour."
  - ✓ Passive (acceptable): "The saree is woven in Molakalmuru." (place is the focus)
  - ✗ Passive (weak): "The tension is adjusted." (by whom? The weaver is important.)

### Negatives

- Avoid double negatives.
- Prefer positive constructions.
  - ✗ "Not uncommon."
  - ✓ "Common."

## Miscellaneous

### Ampersands

- Do not use in body copy.
- Allowed in navigation labels, taglines, and short headings where space is limited.
  - ✓ "Sarees · Stories · You" (navigation)
  - ✗ "The craft & the cloth" (body copy — use "and")

### Abbreviations

- Spell out on first use in any document. Use abbreviation thereafter.
- Avoid abbreviations in narrative sections.
  - ✓ "House of Padmavati (HOP) was founded..." (acceptable first reference with parenthetical)

### Contractions

- Allowed. HOP's voice is warm, not stiff.
  - ✓ "It is woven..." (formal)
  - ✓ "It's woven..." (warm)
- Use contractions consistently within a piece.

### URLs in Copy

- Do not write out URLs in body copy. Use descriptive link text.
  - ✗ "Visit houseofpadmavati.com/journal"
  - ✓ "Read the journal"
