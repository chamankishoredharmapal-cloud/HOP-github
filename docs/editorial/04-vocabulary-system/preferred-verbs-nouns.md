# Preferred Verbs and Nouns

Substitution tables for common writing patterns. When a natural instinct leads to a forbidden or weak word, this document provides the HOP-preferred alternative.

## Transaction → Belonging

Replace verbs of commerce with verbs of relationship and choice.

| Instead of | Use | Context |
|------------|-----|---------|
| Buy | Choose, carry, welcome, bring home | "Choose a weave that belongs to you." |
| Purchase | (avoid as noun; use "order" or "saree") | "Your saree is being wrapped." |
| Sell | Offer, present, release | "We release a handful of weaves each month." |
| Customer | Woman, you, the wearer, our guest | Never "customer" or "consumer." |
| Client | Woman, our guest | Same. |
| Transaction | Order, note, (avoid) | "Your order is confirmed." |
| Checkout | Complete your order | "Complete your order" not "checkout." |
| Payment | Your purchase, your order | Use in functional contexts only. |
| Receipt | Order confirmation, keepsake | Don't lead with "receipt." |
| Refund | Return | "Return" is warmer. "Refund" is legal. |

## Commerce → Curation

Replace mass-market commerce language with editorial, curatorial language.

| Instead of | Use | Context |
|------------|-----|---------|
| Product | Saree, drape, weave, cloth | "Product" is retail language. "Saree" is the real thing. |
| Item | Saree, piece, weave | Same. |
| Catalog | Collection, atelier, the house | "Browse the collection." |
| Inventory | Weaves, current collection, available pieces | "Inventory" is logistics language. |
| Stock | Available, in the atelier | "Currently in the atelier." |
| Restock | A new batch, a new weave | "A new weave from the same loom." |
| Launch | Release, welcome, introduce | "Launch" is tech/product language. |
| Drop | Release | "Drop" is streetwear language. |
| SKU | (never use on storefront) | Internal only. |
| Variant | (avoid) | Use the specific attribute: colour, weave, length. |

## Generic → Specific

Replace generic descriptors with specific, sensory, or technical language.

| Instead of | Use | Context |
|------------|-----|---------|
| Beautiful | (describe the specific quality) | "The zari catches the morning light." |
| Nice | (remove entirely) | Too weak for any context. |
| Good quality | (describe the quality specifically) | "Handwoven in pure mulberry silk." |
| High quality | (same) | Same. |
| Premium | (same) | Never use. |
| Luxury | (same) | Never use. |
| Amazing | (remove) | Internet hyperbole. |
| Special | (describe what makes it special) | "This weave is one of twelve." |
| Unique | (avoid) | Everything is unique. Show it, don't claim it. |
| Iconic | (avoid) | Claims too much. |
| Classic | (avoid unless referring to a specific weave type) | "A classic Ilkal drape." |
| Traditional | (use only with specific technique or region) | "Traditional Molakalmuru temple border." |
| Modern | (use only when contrasting with traditional) | "A modern silhouette on a traditional weave." |

## Passive → Active

Replace passive constructions with active sensory verbs.

| Instead of | Use | Context |
|------------|-----|---------|
| Is made of | Is woven from, is handwoven in | "Woven from pure mulberry silk." |
| Features | Carries, holds, reveals | "The pallu carries a line of hand-spun zari." |
| Has | Carries, holds, settles with | "The border carries temple motifs." |
| Comes with | Includes, arrives with, is accompanied by | "Arrives with a handwritten keepsake card." |
| Designed for | Belongs to, is at home in, was made for | "This drape belongs to winter weddings." |
| Perfect for | Belongs to, is suited to | "Belongs to quiet ceremonies." |
| Ideal for | Same | Same. |
| Suitable for | (avoid — weak) | "This drape for..." |

## Emotion → Precision

Replace abstract emotional language with concrete sensory description.

| Instead of | Use | Context |
|------------|-----|---------|
| Feel the luxury | (describe the sensory experience) | "The weight settles at your hip." |
| Experience the quality | (same) | "The zari does not scratch. It rests against the skin." |
| Love | (reserve for customer's voice, not brand voice) | The brand does not tell you to love something. |
| Fall in love with | (avoid) | Marketing language. |
| Adore | (avoid) | Same. |
| Cherish | (use sparingly, for generational framing) | "A saree to cherish and pass on." |

## Forced → Natural

Replace forced brand constructions with organic language.

| Instead of | Use | Reason |
|------------|-----|--------|
| The HOP difference | (avoid entirely) | Brands that say "the X difference" are trying too hard. |
| At House of Padmavati, we... | (avoid as opening) | Most readers skip brand-name openings. Lead with the story. |
| We believe | (use sparingly) | Show belief through action, not declaration. |
| Our mission | (avoid entirely) | Corporate language. |
| Our vision | (avoid entirely) | Same. |
| Our values | (avoid in copy — document them in the bible) | Show values through writing, not statements. |

## Person → Person

Replace generic references to people with specific, respectful human language.

| Instead of | Use | Context |
|------------|-----|---------|
| The customer | The woman, you, she | Generic → specific. |
| The buyer | Same | Same. |
| The consumer | Same | Never use "consumer." |
| The user | (avoid entirely) | Digital product language, not fashion house language. |
| Artisans | Weavers (preferred), hands, makers | "Artisans" is overused. "Weavers" is specific. |
| Craftspeople | Weavers, dyers, makers | Same. |
| Labour | Work, craft, making | "Labour" reduces human work to economics. |
| Workforce | (avoid) | Industrial language. |
| Staff | (avoid) | Same. |

## Singular → Plural (Community)

HOP speaks as a house, not an individual.

| Instead of | Use | Reason |
|------------|-----|--------|
| I | We, the house | HOP speaks as a collective. |
| My | Our | Same. |
| The founder | The house, we | Avoid third-person founder references. |
| Our founder | (avoid) | Same. |

## Functional Labels (Acceptable Usage)

These words are acceptable in functional contexts (checkout, account, technical specs) but should be avoided in narrative content.

| Word | Allowed In | Not Allowed In |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| Price | Checkout, product detail (functional area) | Narrative, editorial, hero |
| Cost | Checkout, shipping section | Anywhere else |
| Quantity | Cart, checkout | Anywhere else |
| Size | Product detail (functional area) | Narrative |
| Colour | Product detail (functional area) | Use sensory colour description |
| Material | Product detail (accordion) | Narrative description |
