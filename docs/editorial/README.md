# Editorial Operating System

House of Padmavati is not an ecommerce store. It is a digital fashion house where storytelling, craftsmanship, culture, emotion, heritage, and human connection take priority over commerce. Every sentence on every page must serve these priorities.

The Editorial Operating System (EOS) is the permanent governance system for every piece of content HOP creates — website copy, journal articles, product stories, collection narratives, emails, packaging notes, social captions, metadata, microcopy, trust content, policies, and future campaigns. It is designed to enforce consistency at scale across human writers and AI collaborators.

## How to Use This System

1. **Brand first.** Before writing anything, read the Brand Bible and Voice Bible. Every sentence must pass through these documents.

2. **Know the page.** Check the Page Strategy for the page you are writing. Understand its purpose, emotion, and CTAs before choosing words.

3. **Follow the vocabulary.** The approved and forbidden word lists are non-negotiable. When in doubt, consult the grammar and usage guide.

4. **Use the framework.** Each content type has a narrative framework (planned in Phase 2). Follow its block sequence. Do not invent structures.

5. **Design for two systems.** Every piece of content must serve both System 1 (emotional desire) and System 2 (rational justification). The sensory hook arrives first. The trust signals are available on demand.

6. **Preview the emotion.** Use the Emotional Architecture to verify that the feeling a page creates matches its intended place in the customer journey.

7. **Submit to review.** Every piece of content must pass the Editorial Review Checklist before publication. The checklist is defined in the Quality Assurance section (planned).
