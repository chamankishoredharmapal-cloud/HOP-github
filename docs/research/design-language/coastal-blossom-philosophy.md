## Source Attribution

This document expands the Coastal Blossom Philosophy from brand-bible.md into a practical editorial and design framework. The 60-25-10-5 ratio was defined across four workshop sessions between the Editorial Lead and the Design team in May 2026, informed by colour theory from Josef Albers and Faber Birrell, and tested against a full-page audit of HOP's existing content.

## Executive Summary

The Coastal Blossom Philosophy is a compositional and tonal framework expressed as a ratio: 60% space and breath (Jasmine Mist), 25% intention and depth (Coastal Teal), 10% warmth and tradition (Golden Sand), and 5% imperfection and life (Sakura Blush). This ratio applies not only to colour distribution but to every dimension of content — page layout, paragraph pacing, photographic composition, and interaction design. It ensures that every piece of HOP content communicates the same emotional proportions: room to breathe, grounded intention, human warmth, and a trace of the unexpected.

## Key Principles

### Principle 1: 60% is not empty — it is Jasmine Mist

The dominant proportion (60%) is space, quiet, and breath. In visual design it is negative space. In editorial content it is short paragraphs, generous margins, and the absence of noise. This is the primary sensory experience of HOP — the feeling of a room with a window open.

- **Editorial implication:** Articles must reserve 60% of each page for what is not said. This means short paragraphs (3–4 lines max), generous vertical rhythm, and ruthless editing of any sentence that does not earn its place.
- **Example:** A Collection page leads with a single hero image at 60% width, with the collection title and a two-sentence introduction in the remaining 40%. The reader enters through space, not information.

### Principle 2: 25% carries meaning — Coastal Teal

A quarter of the composition is reserved for depth, craft knowledge, and considered navigation. This is the part of the page that does the work — the description of a weave, the provenance of a material, the navigation that guides the reader deeper.

- **Editorial implication:** The 25% is where specificity lives. Craft details, material origins, and technical descriptions belong here. This section must be precise and unhurried, never feature-length but always substantive.
- **Example:** Beneath the hero image (60%), a 25% block contains the collection's design note — three paragraphs on the weave type, the region, and the weaver. It is information, but it is presented with the same calm as the space around it.

### Principle 3: 10% is a hand extended — Golden Sand

Ten percent of the composition carries human warmth — the voice of the weaver, the hands of the woman wearing the saree, the small moment that makes the content feel like a conversation rather than a broadcast.

- **Editorial implication:** This 10% is where quotes, anecdotes, and personal details live. It must feel earned, not inserted. A weaver's one-line reflection carries more weight than a paragraph of brand copy.
- **Example:** In a weaver profile, the weaver's direct quote — "My grandmother taught me this border when I was twelve" — is presented in a serif pull-quote at 10% of the page's visual weight. It is surrounded by space.

### Principle 4: 5% is the crack in the pot — Sakura Blush

The smallest proportion is the most important: imperfection, surprise, life. An unexpected detail — a thread out of place, a child's hand in the frame, a typographic shift, a colour accent. This is what makes HOP feel human rather than polished.

- **Editorial implication:** Every article must contain at least one element that breaks the pattern. This could be an unusually short sentence, an unconventional image crop, or a detail that would be removed in a conventional editorial process.
- **Example:** A product page includes a micro-detail image of a single irregular thread in the zari border — captioned "This is not a flaw. This is the hand." This 5% element is the most memorable part of the page.

## Application to HOP

The ratio is used as a compositional checklist for every new page and article. Before publication, the Editorial Lead scores the page against 60-25-10-5. A page that scores under 40% Jasmine Mist is sent back for revision. The ratio also guides layout templates: the CMS offers pre-built component blocks that correspond to each proportion — a wide-breath hero (60%), a knowledge panel (25%), a quote block (10%), and a detail accent (5%).
