### What zari is

Zari is a metallic thread made by wrapping fine wire around a core fibre. In its purest form, the wire is silver or gold, and the core is pure silk. The silver wire is flattened, wrapped around the silk core in a tight spiral, and then used as weft in the weaving process. The result is a thread that carries light with a particular warmth — not the flat gleam of plastic, but the deep, shifting glow of metal.

### How it is made

Hand-spun zari begins with a silver wire drawn through successively finer holes until it reaches the thickness of a human hair. The wire is then flattened between rollers and wrapped around a silk thread by hand or on a simple spinning wheel.

The process is slow. A single kilogram of hand-spun zari takes days to produce. The irregularities in the wrapping — the slight variations in tension, the places where the wire overlaps or gaps — are not defects. They are the signature of the human hand.

### Real vs imitation

Machine-made zari uses a synthetic core (polyester) wrapped with a metallic-coated film. It is uniform, consistent, and inexpensive. Under a studio light, it looks almost identical to real zari. But in natural light — in the morning light that reveals truth — the difference is unmistakable.

Real zari catches light differently from every angle because the hand-wrapped wire is irregular. It creates small flashes as the light hits each variation. Imitation zari reflects light uniformly — it is bright, but flat. Real zari glows. Imitation zari shines.

Real zari also behaves differently over time. It develops a patina — a soft, subtle darkening that gives an antique quality to the border. Imitation zari either stays the same or peels, revealing the plastic core beneath.

**Visual test:** Real zari has slight irregularities in the wrapping. Look at the thread under natural light — if every part of the thread reflects identically, it is imitation.

**Weight test:** Real zari is heavier than imitation. A saree with real zari will feel noticeably heavier in the border and pallu.

**Flex test:** Real zari is malleable — it bends and creases without cracking. Imitation zari develops sharp creases where the metallic film cracks.

**Time test:** Real zari develops a patina over years. Imitation zari peels, tarnishes unevenly, or stays unchanged.
