# System 1 and System 2 — Dual-Process Theory in Editorial Design

## Source Attribution

This document synthesises the dual-process theory of cognition as developed by Daniel Kahneman and extended by Evans, Stanovich, and West. The core framework — System 1 (fast, intuitive, emotional) and System 2 (slow, analytical, rational) — is drawn from Kahneman's *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (2011). The application to editorial and ecommerce contexts is original to House of Padmavati.

## Executive Summary

System 1 and System 2 describe two modes of thinking: the fast, intuitive, emotional mind and the slow, analytical, rational mind. For a premium purchase like a handwoven saree, both systems must be satisfied — System 1 must feel desire, and System 2 must find justification. HOP editorial is designed to alternate between these modes, using sensory language to engage System 1 and verifiable facts to satisfy System 2, never allowing one to dominate the other.

## Key Principles

### 1. Alternating Engagement

**Definition:** Content must alternate between System 1 hooks (sensory, emotional, immediate) and System 2 anchors (verifiable, specific, rational). Neither mode can sustain attention alone. System 1 without System 2 feels like manipulation. System 2 without System 1 feels like a textbook.

**Editorial implication:** Every content unit — product description, journal article, collection story — must contain at least one System 1 trigger (sensory language, emotional resonance) and one System 2 anchor (specific fact, verifiable claim, named source). The alternation should be rhythmic, not mechanical.

**Example:** "The zari catches the morning light" (System 1 — sensory, emotional) followed by "Pure zari, hand-spun, genuine — tested and certified" (System 2 — verifiable, specific).

### 2. Sensory Primacy

**Definition:** System 1 processes sensory information faster and more deeply than abstract information. The brain simulates sensory experiences through mirror neurons, creating a feeling of virtual possession before any rational decision is made.

**Editorial implication:** Every product description must lead with sensory language — weight, drape, colour behaviour, sound, texture. Technical specifications must be available but secondary, placed in accordion sections that the reader can choose to open. The sensory experience is the primary content; the rational verification is the supporting content.

**Example:** "The fabric settles at the hip with a weight that says 'I am here'" (System 1 — sensory simulation) before "Pure mulberry silk, 12-momme, hand-dyed in small batches" (System 2 — verifiable specification).

### 3. Rational Anchoring

**Definition:** System 2 requires verifiable evidence before committing to a high-involvement purchase. Without rational anchors, emotional desire collapses when the reader encounters the price. The rational mind must be able to justify the emotional decision.

**Editorial implication:** Every product page must provide System 2 content on demand — weave specifications, fabric composition, care instructions, origin details, dispatch information. This content must be specific, verifiable, and presented without marketing inflation. The reader's rational mind should find exactly what it needs to justify the purchase.

**Example:** A product page leads with "The weight settles at your hip like a held breath" (System 1) and makes available via accordion: "Pure mulberry silk, 12-momme, hand-dyed in small batches. Woven on a pit loom in Molakalmuru, Karnataka. 5.5 metres including pallu. Dispatch within 7 business days." (System 2).

## Application to HOP

**Content structure rule:** Every content unit must follow a System 1 → System 2 rhythm. Lead with sensory or emotional language. Follow with verifiable fact. Never reverse this order — leading with specifications creates a clinical tone that suppresses desire.

**Page-level application:**
- **Product Detail:** Sensory description in the hero section (System 1). Accordion with weave specifications, care instructions, origin (System 2).
- **Journal articles:** Emotional narrative in the body (System 1). Source citations and verifiable claims in the text or footnotes (System 2).
- **Collection stories:** Evocative naming and taglines (System 1). Specific region, technique, and weaver details (System 2).
- **Homepage:** Silent video and poetic tagline (System 1). Craft section with specific numbers — "sixty hours per drape" (System 2).

**Editorial review rule:** Before publishing any content, identify the System 1 elements and the System 2 elements. If either is missing, the content is incomplete. If the System 2 elements are not verifiable, the content is not ready.
