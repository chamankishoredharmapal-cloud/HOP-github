## Source Attribution

This topic architecture was derived from a full content audit of hop.luxe conducted on 15 June 2026, a competitive content mapping exercise across 5 direct and adjacent brands, and an internal gap analysis using Ahrefs to identify high-opportunity, zero-saturation topics.

## Executive Summary

House of Padmavati's content must be a resource for a lifetime of wearing handloom, not a catalogue. Four pillar topics anchor the editorial strategy — Handloom Education, Saree Care & Rituals, Weaver Stories, and Collection Narratives. Each pillar contains 3–5 cluster subtopics, and each cluster maps to specific customer intents. Three content gaps have been identified on the current site that represent immediate priorities.

## Key Principles

### Principle 1: Pillars before posts

Every article belongs to exactly one pillar. No orphan content. Pillars are evergreen; clusters are refreshed annually.

- **Editorial implication:** Before writing any piece, the Editorial Lead must confirm its pillar home. A piece that does not fit a pillar is either a new pillar or not worth writing.
- **Example:** An article on "washing a chanderi saree" belongs to Saree Care & Rituals, not Handloom Education, because the intent is maintenance, not craft knowledge.

### Principle 2: Cluster authority

A pillar page must link meaningfully to its clusters, and clusters must link back to the pillar. Internal linking is the editorial infrastructure of expertise.

- **Editorial implication:** Every cluster article must contain at least one contextual link to its parent pillar page. The pillar page must aggregate and introduce cluster content.
- **Example:** The Handloom Education pillar links to clusters like "What is Mulberry Silk," "Types of Handloom Weaves," and "How to Identify Real Zari."

### Principle 3: Gap-first creation

New content is prioritised by gap size, not by convenience. If a high-intent topic has no coverage, it takes precedence over a topic with partial coverage.

- **Editorial implication:** The editorial calendar is driven by the gap analysis, not by writer preference or seasonal convenience.
- **Example:** "Saree storage for humid climates" (0 articles) is scheduled before "How to choose a wedding saree" (1 existing article, partial coverage).

## Identified Content Gaps

| Gap | Suggested pillar | Urgency |
|---|---|---|
| No content on saree storage / care — highest-search care topic on Ahrefs with zero HOP coverage | Saree Care & Rituals | Immediate |
| No weaver portrait content — "who made my saree" is a top-5 internal search query | Weaver Stories | Immediate |
| No region-by-region weaving guide — high informational volume, zero competition from premium brands | Handloom Education | Short-term |

## Application to HOP

The editorial calendar is restructured around these four pillars. Each quarter, one pillar is designated as the "deep-dive pillar" receiving 3 new cluster articles, while the others receive 1 each. The gap analysis is reviewed every 60 days. The Pillar → Cluster → Article hierarchy replaces the current flat article structure. All existing articles are being retrofitted into pillars with updated internal links.
