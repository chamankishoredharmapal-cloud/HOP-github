## Source Attribution

This document synthesises keyword research conducted by the Editorial Lead in June 2026 using Ahrefs, Google Search Console (verified data for hop.luxe, n=342 queries), SEMrush competitor gap analysis of 10 premium saree brands, and a manual audit of top-of-funnel content across handloom-focussed publishers.

## Executive Summary

House of Padmavati will not chase high-volume, low-intent keywords. The strategy targets three narrow intent bands — transactional, informational, and navigational — with distinct keyword clusters for each. Every keyword must pass a "quiet luxury" filter: does this search term align with a customer who values authenticity, slowness, and craft knowledge over speed and discount?

## Key Principles

### Principle 1: Intent over volume

A keyword's value is determined not by monthly search volume but by its alignment with the purchase journey of a high-consideration, craft-aware customer.

- **Editorial implication:** Never optimise for a keyword whose intent contradicts the brand. For example, "cheap silk saree" will rank 0 in our content, regardless of volume.
- **Example:** "How to identify real zari" (300 searches/mo) is worth more than "saree online" (12,000 searches/mo) because the former signals a discerning buyer.

### Principle 2: Cluster by customer journey stage

Keywords are organised by whether the customer is discovering, evaluating, or ready to transact. Content type follows intent.

- **Editorial implication:** Every article must serve exactly one intent. An informational article must not contain transactional calls-to-action, and vice versa.
- **Example:** "What is mulberry silk" (informational) → Journal pillar article. "Buy handwoven saree online" (transactional) → Collection page with filtering.

### Principle 3: Long-tail as signals of trust

Long-tail keyword queries — "how to store kanjivaram saree during monsoon" — indicate a customer who trusts the brand enough to ask deeper questions. These drive the highest conversion rates.

- **Editorial implication:** Each long-tail cluster deserves its own dedicated article or service page. Do not consolidate multiple long-tail queries into one thin page.
- **Example:** "How to repurpose a pattu saree" becomes a complete Journal article with styled photos and weaver interview.

## Application to HOP

Keyword research feeds directly into the editorial calendar. Each month, the Editorial Lead selects one transactional, two informational (one pillar, one cluster), and one long-tail keyword to brief. All new content is cross-checked against the keyword clusters above to avoid cannibalisation. Existing content is audited quarterly for keyword alignment; pages serving the wrong intent are archived or rewritten.
