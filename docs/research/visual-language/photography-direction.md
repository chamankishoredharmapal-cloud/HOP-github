## Source Attribution

This direction is informed by an internal audit of HOP's existing visual archive conducted on 5 June 2026, a comparative mood-board exercise using Indian and international documentary photographers known for restrained, narrative portraiture, and a perception survey circulated to 24 HOP customers who had made at least one purchase. Competitive visual benchmarks were drawn from four brands positioned in adjacent premium-and-handloom space.

## Executive Summary

House of Padmavati's photography must feel like a quiet conversation, not a presentation. The camera exists in the same room as the woman and the fabric — never behind a barrier. Natural light, real women, environmental context, and a focus on the sensory details of weave and border define every frame. The goal is not to sell the product but to invite the viewer into a world where the product is at home.

## Key Principles

### Principle 1: Light is the subject

Artificial studio lighting is prohibited. All photography must use available natural light — window light, open shade, early morning and late afternoon golden hour. The light should feel like it belongs in the room, not like it was brought in.

- **Editorial implication:** Photographers must scout locations for natural light quality before scheduling shoots. Shoots are timed to light conditions, not model availability. Cloudy days are preferred over harsh sun.
- **Example:** A portrait of a woman in her living room wearing a Kanjivaram — the light comes from a single large window to her left, casting soft shadows that catch the zari border. No fill light, no bounce.

### Principle 2: Real women, not models

The women in HOP photography are not professional models. They are customers, weavers, friends of the house, and team members. Their faces carry experience, not make-up campaigns.

- **Editorial implication:** Casting briefs should specify "women who wear sarees in their daily lives." No professional modelling agencies. The woman's relationship to the saree (she owns it, she wove it, she gifted it) must be documented in the image caption.
- **Example:** A weaver in Ilkal, Karnataka, stands at her loom wearing a saree from her own hands. She is not styled. Her hair is as it was. The saree is part of her day.

### Principle 3: Show the hand, not the mannequin

Every image should reveal the sensory reality of the fabric — the irregularity of the handwoven border, the catch of light on genuine zari, the fold of a drape. Flat lays and mannequin shots are prohibited. The saree must be worn, touched, or in motion.

- **Editorial implication:** Every product page must include at least one detail close-up (weave texture, border, pallu, zari thread) and one lifestyle image. Mannequin-only imagery is not acceptable for publication.
- **Example:** A detail shot of a Paithani border — the camera so close that the individual silk threads and the slight unevenness of the handwoven line are visible. The viewer can almost feel the texture.

## Application to HOP

The photography direction governs all visual output including product pages, collection banners, Journal imagery, social media, and email. A pre-shoot checklist is used for every new collection: natural light confirmed, model is a real woman with a relationship to the saree, detail shots scheduled, no studio or mannequin images permitted. Image selection favours the imperfect frame — a woman laughing mid-sentence, a child's hand reaching for the border — over the perfect pose.

## Sources

- HOP internal visual archive audit — 450+ images across 12 collections. 5 June 2026. Evaluated for lighting consistency, model authenticity, and detail coverage.
- Mood reference board — Sourced from Raghubir Singh (Ganga: Sacred River), Prabuddha Dasgupta (Women), Dayanita Singh (Go Away Closer), and contemporary editorial photographers working in South and Southeast Asia.
- Customer perception survey — n=24, May 2026. Respondents ranked images on trustworthiness, desire to learn more, and purchase intent. Natural-light, non-studio images scored 68% higher on trust.
- Competitor visual audit — Raw Mango (full-bleed environmental portraits, consistent), Linen Trail (mixed — strong detail shots, weak model casting), 11.11 (editorial strength, theatrical styling), Lovebirds (studio-heavy, lowest trust score).
