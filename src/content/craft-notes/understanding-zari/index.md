---
type: intro
---

Zari is the metallic thread that turns a woven saree into something that catches light. But not all zari is the same — and the difference between hand-spun and machine-made, between real and imitation, is the difference between a saree that glows and one that merely shines.

---
type: body
---

Real zari begins as a thin silver wire. This wire is flattened, then wrapped around a silk core thread. The result is flexible enough to weave but dense enough to catch light. The process is entirely by hand — the spinner draws the wire through a series of progressively finer holes until it reaches the desired thickness. Each pass reduces the diameter. Each pass risks breaking the wire. A single mistake means starting over.

After the silver wire is spun around the silk core, it is washed in gold. This gives zari its warm colour and prevents the silver from tarnishing against the skin. The gold layer is measured in microns — too thick and the thread becomes stiff, too thin and it wears off. The balance requires generations of experience.

---

Imitation zari replaces silver with copper and gold with a lacquer coating. It is cheaper, faster, and indistinguishable to the untrained eye. But it behaves differently over time. Real zari softens with age — the silk core relaxes, the metal develops a patina that deepens rather than fades. Imitation zari stiffens. The lacquer flakes. The thread loses its flexibility.

The difference is not visible on day one. It reveals itself over years. This is why real zari is an investment and imitation zari is not — one improves with time, the other declines.

---
type: system-2
title: "How to identify real zari"
---

1. **Flex test:** Real zari bends easily. Imitation zari resists.
2. **Weight test:** Real zari is heavier — silver is denser than copper.
3. **Edge test:** Look at the edge of the thread. Real zari shows a silver core. Imitation shows copper (reddish).
4. **Age test:** A saree worn for 5+ years with real zari will have softer, more lustrous zari. Imitation will show wear at points of friction.

---
type: closure
---

When you buy a saree with hand-spun, real zari, you are not buying a thread. You are buying a technique that has been refined over centuries, a material that will outlive its first wearer, and a glow that cannot be manufactured. The zari knows the difference. Over time, you will too.
