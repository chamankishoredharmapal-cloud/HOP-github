term: "Zari"
slug: zari
pronunciation: "/ˈzɑːri/"
category: Material
definition: "A metallic thread traditionally made of fine silver or gold wire wrapped around a silk core, used in Indian handloom weaving to create decorative patterns and borders."
extended: "Real zari is made by spinning silver wire around a silk core, then washing it in gold. The result is flexible enough to weave but dense enough to catch light. Imitation zari uses copper and lacquer — cheaper, but stiffer and prone to tarnishing. The difference between real and imitation zari reveals itself over years, not days."
